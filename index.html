My personal experience with meditation

Hi, my name is Daniel Gage and I'm here to tell you how mediation can positively help with your mental health.
For me, my mental health started to decline when my gap year in New Zealand was coming to an end. I was facing the reality of going back to a Country which I didn't want to be in, having no idea what career I would like to do and having to leave a Country which I fell in love with. It was affecting the job I was in at the time, my relationship and overall well-being. I felt like I wanted to escape and run away.
I knew things had to change within myself but I had no idea how!
I have always been the type of person to hide my feelings and just get on with life, however, after being back in the U.K. for 6 months, things were only getting worse and I had a complete breakdown. I didn't want to be alive anymore and I knew it was time I needed help.
The Doctors gave me antidepressants, which helped to begin with and still do, to this day. I started online counselling but I felt like I couldn't relate to anyone so I started looking into mediation and mindfulness.

Learning mediation was hard but I persevered with the help of the Headspace app.
I first felt different after a week or so, I wasn't waking up in such a bad mood which drastically changed my day to day life. I felt alot happier about my life and it was the push that I needed to make a change in my life. Yes, I still have bad days or weeks sometimes but if I keep up with my daily meditation, I find that it really helps.
I enrolled onto a Web and Software Development course, me and my partner brought a van and converted it into a Campervan (DIY STYLE) and now plan to both get remote jobs, to enable us to travel and work around the world!

The truth is, you can seek help, through councelling, family and friends but the only person that can truly help you see the other side is YOURSELF
