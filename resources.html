The link between meditation and mental well-being


How Meditation can help mental health
Who can practice Meditation
How to practice Meditation
  • Reduces stress, anxiety and depression
  • Increased awareness and compassion
  • Brings a sense of calm
  • Recharges the brain

    • Anyone can meditate, no matter of gender,age,religion or nationality.
    Some peope might not take to it as quick as others,
    but if you persevere you will start noticing little changes in yourself.

  • Find a comfortable location, away from all distractions
  • Wear comfortable clothes
  • You can sit down with your feet on the floor,
    kneel or even lie down in bed
  • Set a time limit
  • Close your eyes and begin by taking some deep breaths
  • Listen to your mind, if it wanders.
    Slowly bring it back to focusing on the present
  • Be kind to yourself

    Guided Meditation

    Guided meditation is basically meditation led by a Teacher, either in person or via audio and video.

    Some of the resources I used on my journey was Headspace and Wysa both are very different.


    Headspace

    Headspace is a mindfulness app, it has a variety of courses that you can complete depending on what you need to focus on.

    You can access meditation courses, ranging from; What is Meditation? to Meditation Techniques.

    There are also sleep and stress sessions, these can help with falling back to sleep, how to deep sleep, how to wake up
    how to relieve stress, how to relax and how to anxiety.

    These are just a few courses you can complete through the Headspace app.

    Wysa

    Wysa is a Artifical intelligence coaching app, where you remain annonymous and you can express your feelings without being judged.

    On the Wysa app, there are guided meditation sessions, with issues ranging from Trauma to Essential Wellness.

    There are also options for self-care, where you work on challenges by yourself or you can go for guided support
    there are links to therapists and it uses your location to find local therapists.

    There is a journal section, which I used the most. If I ever had thoughts that I didn't want to speak about, I would journal these down,
    you find yourself being able to think more clearly once you start expressing how your feeling, it doesn't matter how.

    Check out Headspace here! Check out Wysa here!

    Youtube and Podcasts

    Some of the podcasts that I listen to on a daily basis are The Mindset Mentor and Let's talk about mental health


    The Mindset Mentor

    For me The Mindset Mentor helps drastically when I'm having a rough day and I need that positive boost, he's also highly motivated
    which is very addictive. He makes you want to succeed and push through the rough times and I find that hearing that from a stranger is always more thought provoking
    than speaking to a family member or friend.

    Let's talk about mental health

    Let's talk about mental health is a podcast about dealing with all aspects of emotions, feelings and mental health.

    He talks about his own experience and he also talks about what helped him,
    obviously everyone has different issues but I'm sure there will be at least one episode that you can relate to.
